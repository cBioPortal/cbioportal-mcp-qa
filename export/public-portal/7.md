Based on my analysis of the cBioPortal data, here are the **top 5 most frequently altered genes in structural variants in Osteosarcoma**:

## Top 5 Most Frequently Altered Genes in Osteosarcoma Structural Variants

**Study**: Soft Tissue and Bone Sarcoma (MSK, Nat Commun 2022)

| Rank | Gene Symbol | Samples with Alterations | Description |
|------|-------------|-------------------------|-------------|
| 1 | **EWSR1** | 35 samples | Ewing Sarcoma Breakpoint Region 1 |
| 2 | **SS18** | 15 samples | Synovial Sarcoma Translocation, Chromosome 18 |
| 3 | **FLI1** | 13 samples | Fli-1 Proto-Oncogene, ETS Transcription Factor |
| 4 | **FUS** | 11 samples | FUS RNA Binding Protein |
| 4 | **DDIT3** | 11 samples | DNA Damage Inducible Transcript 3 |

### Key Findings:

- **Total osteosarcoma samples in study**: 460
- **Samples with structural variants**: 224 (48.7%)
- **Data source**: MSK-IMPACT sequencing from Memorial Sloan Kettering

### Clinical Significance:

1. **EWSR1**: Most frequently altered gene, commonly involved in sarcoma translocations, particularly in Ewing sarcoma family tumors
2. **SS18**: Associated with synovial sarcoma translocations
3. **FLI1**: Often found as a fusion partner with EWSR1 in Ewing sarcoma
4. **FUS** and **DDIT3**: Both involved in myxoid liposarcoma translocations

**Important Note**: While these genes show high frequency of structural alterations in the osteosarcoma samples, some of these fusion patterns (like EWSR1-FLI1) are more characteristic of other sarcoma subtypes, suggesting potential diagnostic complexity or mixed histology in some cases within this large clinical dataset.
