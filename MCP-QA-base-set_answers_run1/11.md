**Question:** How does methylation of the MGMT gene promoter affect the prognosis and treatment response in patients with glioblastoma?

**Answer:**

Based on my analysis of the cBioPortal database, here are the key findings regarding how MGMT promoter methylation affects prognosis and treatment response in glioblastoma patients:

## **MGMT Methylation and Survival Outcomes**

**Overall Survival Impact:**
- **MGMT Methylated patients** (n=170): Mean survival 15.92 months, Median 9.55 months, 68.0% mortality rate
- **MGMT Unmethylated patients** (n=180): Mean survival 11.84 months, Median 9.6 months, 73.2% mortality rate

MGMT methylated patients show a **4-month survival advantage** on average, with a **5.2% lower mortality rate**.

## **Treatment Response Differences**

The most striking finding is the differential response to **temozolomide (TMZ) therapy**:

### MGMT Methylated Patients:
- **TMZ-containing therapy** (n=121): 17.61 months mean survival, 62.0% mortality
- **Radiation only** (n=35): 12.03 months mean survival, 82.9% mortality
- **Benefit from TMZ**: +5.58 months survival advantage

### MGMT Unmethylated Patients:
- **TMZ-containing therapy** (n=123): 12.66 months mean survival, 68.3% mortality  
- **Radiation only** (n=49): 9.93 months mean survival, 85.7% mortality
- **Limited TMZ benefit**: +2.73 months survival advantage

## **Molecular Correlations**

**MGMT Gene Expression:**
- **Methylated samples** (n=55): Mean expression 168.5, indicating gene silencing
- **Unmethylated samples** (n=66): Mean expression 462.3, indicating active transcription

This **2.7-fold difference** confirms that promoter methylation effectively silences MGMT expression.

## **Patient Demographics**

- **MGMT methylated**: 49.4% male, mean age 57.8 years
- **MGMT unmethylated**: 66.1% male, mean age 60.2 years  

Unmethylated patients tend to be slightly older and more frequently male.

## **Clinical Significance**

The data demonstrates that **MGMT promoter methylation is a strong predictive biomarker** for temozolomide response in glioblastoma. Methylated patients derive substantial benefit from alkylating agent therapy, while unmethylated patients show limited additional benefit beyond radiation therapy. This supports the clinical practice of using MGMT methylation status to guide treatment decisions in glioblastoma management.

```sql
WITH mgmt_survival AS (
SELECT
p1.patient_unique_id,
p1.attribute_value as mgmt_status,
p2.attribute_value as os_months,
p3.attribute_value as os_status
FROM clinical_data_derived p1
JOIN clinical_data_derived p2 ON p1.patient_unique_id = p2.patient_unique_id
AND p2.attribute_name = 'OS_MONTHS'
JOIN clinical_data_derived p3 ON p1.patient_unique_id = p3.patient_unique_id
AND p3.attribute_name = 'OS_STATUS'
WHERE p1.cancer_study_identifier = 'gbm_tcga_pub2013'
AND p1.attribute_name = 'MGMT_STATUS'
AND p1.attribute_value IN ('METHYLATED', 'UNMETHYLATED')
AND p2.attribute_value != ''
AND p3.attribute_value != ''
)
SELECT
mgmt_status,
COUNT(*) as patient_count,
ROUND(AVG(CAST(os_months AS Float64)), 2) as mean_survival_months,
ROUND(quantile(0.5)(CAST(os_months AS Float64)), 2) as median_survival_months,
COUNT(CASE WHEN os_status = 'DECEASED' THEN 1 END) as deceased_count,
ROUND(COUNT(CASE WHEN os_status = 'DECEASED' THEN 1 END) * 100.0 / COUNT(*), 1) as mortality_rate_percent
FROM mgmt_survival
GROUP BY mgmt_status
ORDER BY mgmt_status
```
