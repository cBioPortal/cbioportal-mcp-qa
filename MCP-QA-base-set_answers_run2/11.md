**Question:** How does methylation of the MGMT gene promoter affect the prognosis and treatment response in patients with glioblastoma?

## MGMT Promoter Methylation and Glioblastoma Outcomes

### Key Findings from TCGA Data:

**1. Survival Benefit of MGMT Methylation:**
- **MGMT methylated patients** (n=169): 
  - Average overall survival: 16.0 months
  - Mortality rate: 68.0%
- **MGMT unmethylated patients** (n=179):
  - Average overall survival: 11.9 months  
  - Mortality rate: 73.2%

**2. Treatment Response Patterns:**
The most significant finding relates to temozolomide (TMZ) treatment response:

**TMZ Chemoradiation + TMZ Chemotherapy:**
- **MGMT methylated**: 83 patients, median survival 11.5 months, 56.6% mortality
- **MGMT unmethylated**: 80 patients, median survival 10.7 months, 65.0% mortality

**3. Broader Glioma Context (LGG/GBM cohort, n=861):**
- **MGMT methylated**: Average survival 21.9 months, 31.2% mortality
- **MGMT unmethylated**: Average survival 14.9 months, 59.1% mortality

**4. Interaction with IDH Status:**
MGMT methylation shows different prognostic value depending on IDH mutation status:
- **MGMT methylated + IDH mutant**: 26.5 months average survival, 15.3% mortality
- **MGMT methylated + IDH wildtype**: 12.3 months average survival, 61.1% mortality
- **MGMT unmethylated + IDH wildtype**: 13.3 months average survival, 62.4% mortality

### Clinical Implications:

**1. Prognostic Value:** MGMT promoter methylation is associated with improved overall survival in glioblastoma patients, with methylated tumors showing approximately 4 months longer survival on average.

**2. Predictive Value for TMZ:** Patients with MGMT methylated tumors appear to derive greater benefit from temozolomide-based therapy, showing lower mortality rates compared to unmethylated tumors when treated with TMZ.

**3. Molecular Context Matters:** The prognostic impact of MGMT methylation is most pronounced in IDH-wildtype tumors (typical GBM), while IDH-mutant status itself confers a strong survival advantage regardless of MGMT status.

This data supports the clinical use of MGMT promoter methylation testing as both a prognostic biomarker and a predictive marker for temozolomide sensitivity in glioblastoma management.
