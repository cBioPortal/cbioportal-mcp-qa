1. **Prognostic Value**: MGMT promoter methylation is a significant positive prognostic factor in glioblastoma, associated with longer overall and disease-free survival.

2. **Treatment Selection**: MGMT methylated patients derive greater benefit from temozolomide-based chemotherapy, with the combination of standard radiation and TMZ showing the best outcomes.

3. **Personalized Medicine**: MGMT status should be considered alongside other molecular markers (IDH1, G-CIMP) for optimal treatment stratification.

4. **Treatment Resistance**: MGMT unmethylated tumors show inherent resistance to alkylating agents like temozolomide, suggesting the need for alternative therapeutic approaches.

The data strongly supports MGMT promoter methylation testing as a standard biomarker for glioblastoma treatment planning, with methylated tumors showing both better prognosis and enhanced response to temozolomide-based therapy.
